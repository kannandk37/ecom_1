All 19 Flows — User Walkthrough Stories
Think of this as a day in the life of your warehouse system. Real people, real actions, real decisions.

Flow 1: Product Onboarding into a Bin
"The truck just arrived"
It's 9am. Ravi, the warehouse manager at Warehouse A in Chennai, gets a call — the supplier truck has arrived with a fresh shipment of Vitamin C Tablets, 500mg.
He opens the warehouse app on his tablet. He goes to Inbound Receiving and selects the Purchase Order that was raised earlier. He walks to Aisle 2, Rack 3, Level 1 — that's where Vitamin C is stored. He scans the bin — it reads A2R3L1.
He enters:

Batch Number: BATCH-2025-001
Expiry Date: June 2027
Quantity: 180 units
Manufactured Date: January 2025

Before accepting, the system silently checks — "Does this batch have at least 180 days of shelf life remaining?" June 2027 is 25 months away. ✅ Passes.
The system then checks — "Does this bin have enough room?" A2R3L1 has a max of 200 units and currently has 10 units sitting there. 10 + 180 = 190. ✅ Fits.
Stock is accepted. A BinStock record is created — 180 units, BATCH-2025-001, expiry June 2027, sitting in A2R3L1. The Inventory record for Vitamin C updates — total stock goes up by 180. The StockLedger logs an INBOUND_RECEIVE entry.
Ravi walks away. The product is now in the system.

Flow 2: Threshold Check & Reorder
"We're running low — but are we really?"
Three months later. Orders have been flowing. The Vitamin C stock in A2R3L1 has dropped from 180 to 18 units.
The moment the 19th unit was picked for an order, the system checked automatically — "Is bin A2R3L1 below its minimum threshold of 20?" Yes. It is.
But before raising any alarm, the system asks a smarter question — "What's the total Vitamin C stock across ALL bins in this warehouse?"
It finds:

A2R3L1 → 18 units
A2R4L2 → 95 units
A3R1L1 → 60 units

Total: 173 units. The warehouse-level reorder point is 50 units. 173 is well above 50.
No purchase order is raised. Instead, the system notes that A2R3L1 is individually low and flags it for an internal stock transfer suggestion — pull some stock from A2R4L2 to replenish A2R3L1.
Now imagine a different scenario — all three bins are nearly empty. Total stock drops to 45 units, below the reorder point of 50. Now the system fires. It checks — "Is there already a pending Purchase Order for Vitamin C?" No. So it automatically creates one and sends Ravi a notification: "Vitamin C stock is critically low. A reorder of 200 units has been triggered. Please review and confirm."
Ravi opens the app, reviews the PO, and confirms it. The supplier is notified.

Flow 3: Customer Order — Batch Selection
"Which batch do we ship?"
Priya in Bangalore orders 10 units of Vitamin C Tablets. She's 2 days away by courier.
The system kicks into action. It calculates — "Priya is 2 days away. Our minimum shelf life buffer is 90 days. So the batch we pick must expire no earlier than today + 2 + 90 = 92 days from now."
It queries all BinStocks for Vitamin C and finds:

BATCH-2025-001 → 18 units → Expires June 2026 → Only 30 days away ❌ FAILS
BATCH-2025-002 → 95 units → Expires March 2027 → 10 months away ✅ PASSES

The system picks 10 units from BATCH-2025-002. It marks them as reserved — qtyReserved goes up by 10, qtyOnHand goes down by 10 in that BinStock. The StockLedger logs a RESERVATION entry.
A pick list is generated for the warehouse staff — "Go to A2R4L2, pick 10 units of BATCH-2025-002 for Order #ORD-5521."
The staff picks the items, packs them, and marks the order as dispatched. The system logs an OUTBOUND entry. Stock is permanently deducted. Threshold check runs again automatically.

Flow 4: PIN Code Delivery Eligibility
"Can we even deliver this to you?"
Karthik in the Andaman Islands opens the product page for Vitamin C Tablets. He types his PIN code 744101 before adding to cart.
The system checks with the courier API — "Is 744101 serviceable?" Yes, but delivery takes 18 days.
Now the system calculates — "18 days delivery + 90 days buffer = batch must expire after 108 days from today."
It scans all BinStocks for Vitamin C:

BATCH-2025-001 → Expires in 30 days ❌
BATCH-2025-002 → Expires in 10 months ✅ and has 85 units available

The page shows — "Delivery available by [date 18 days from now]" and Karthik can add to cart.
Now imagine a different customer — Suresh in a remote Himalayan village, 30 days delivery. Buffer needed: 120 days. Only BATCH-2025-001 is left with 30 days — fails. No other batch. The page shows — "Sorry, this product cannot be delivered to your location before its expiry date." Suresh cannot order. No exception, no workaround.

Flow 5: Multi-Bin Fulfillment
"One bin isn't enough"
A hospital in Mumbai places a bulk order — 120 units of Vitamin C Tablets. Delivery is 3 days away. Buffer needed: 93 days.
The system checks all valid BinStocks sorted by expiry (earliest first, but must pass the 93-day cutoff):

A2R4L2 → BATCH-2025-002 → 85 units ✅
A3R1L1 → BATCH-2025-002 → 60 units ✅

85 units covers the first chunk. Still need 35 more. System moves to A3R1L1 and takes 35 from there.
Total: 85 + 35 = 120 units. ✅ Order fulfilled across two bins.
Two separate RESERVATION ledger entries are created. Two separate lines on the pick list — warehouse staff goes to A2R4L2 first, picks 85, then walks to A3R1L1, picks 35, packs together as one order.

Flow 6: Internal Stock Transfer (Replenishment)
"Move stock, don't order new"
Back to the earlier scenario. A2R3L1 has only 18 units. The total warehouse stock is 173 units — no need to reorder. But A2R3L1 is running dry and it's a frequently picked bin.
Ravi gets a suggestion notification — "Bin A2R3L1 is low. Bin A2R4L2 has 95 units of the same product. Consider transferring 50 units."
Ravi approves. The system creates a StockTransfer record. A warehouse staff member walks to A2R4L2, picks 50 units of BATCH-2025-002, carries them to A2R3L1, and scans the destination bin to confirm.
The system:

Deducts 50 from A2R4L2's BinStock → logs TRANSFER_OUT
Adds 50 to A2R3L1's BinStock → logs TRANSFER_IN
Updates both bins' currentStock

A2R3L1 is replenished. No supplier involved. No money spent. Just smart internal movement.

Flow 7: Customer Return
"Priya sent it back"
Priya received her Vitamin C but changed her mind. She initiates a return on the app for 3 units.
The system traces back to her original order. It knows she was shipped from BATCH-2025-002, which expires March 2027. Today the remaining shelf life of that batch is 9 months.
The system checks — "Is 9 months >= the 90-day minimum buffer to restock?" Yes. ✅
The 3 units are cleared for restocking. Ravi's team receives the returned package, inspects it, confirms packaging is intact, and scans it back into bin A2R3L1.
The system:

Adds 3 units back to BATCH-2025-002's BinStock in A2R3L1
Logs RETURN_RECEIVE in the StockLedger
Updates bin currentStock and Inventory totalStock

Now imagine the return was from a batch expiring in 20 days. The system flags it — "Shelf life too low to restock." The units go directly to quarantine instead. Flow 10 takes over.

Flow 8: Expired Stock Sweep
"The midnight cleanup"
Every night at midnight, a background job quietly runs across the entire system.
Tonight it finds — BATCH-2025-001 in bin A2R3L1 has 18 units remaining and its expiry date was yesterday. It's officially expired.
The system:

Creates a QuarantineRecord for those 18 units, reason: EXPIRED
Sets BinStock isActive = false
Deducts 18 from bin.currentStock
Deducts 18 from Inventory.totalStock
Logs EXPIRED_WRITE_OFF in StockLedger

Ravi gets a morning notification — "18 units of Vitamin C, BATCH-2025-001 in bin A2R3L1 have expired and been written off."
Then the system immediately runs a warehouse-level reorder check. Total stock is now 155 units. Still above reorder point of 50. No PO triggered.
But Ravi now has a decision to make on those 18 expired units — dispose them physically and close the QuarantineRecord.

Flow 9: Physical Stock Audit
"The numbers don't match"
Every month, Ravi does a physical count. He walks to A2R4L2 with a clipboard. He counts BATCH-2025-002 — he physically sees 79 units. But the system says 85 units.
Discrepancy: -6 units.
He opens the Stock Audit screen in the app, selects the bin and batch, enters 79 as physical count. The system shows him the gap — "System: 85 | Physical: 79 | Discrepancy: -6."
Ravi enters a reason — "6 units found damaged during count, moved to quarantine."
The system:

Creates a StockAudit record
Adjusts BinStock qtyOnHand to 79
Logs CYCLE_COUNT_ADJUST in StockLedger with -6
Triggers threshold check — total stock is now 167, still above reorder point

Those 6 damaged units flow into Flow 10.

Flow 10: Damaged Stock / Quarantine
"These can't be sold"
During receiving or handling, a staff member drops a box. 4 units of BATCH-2025-002 are visibly damaged — torn packaging, broken tablets.
He opens the app and flags those 4 units as damaged in the BinStock record.
The system:

Deducts 4 from qtyOnHand, adds 4 to qtyDamaged in that BinStock
Creates a QuarantineRecord — reason: DAMAGED
Logs QUARANTINE in StockLedger

The QuarantineRecord sits with status QUARANTINED. Now Ravi has three options:
Option A — Dispose: Units are physically destroyed. Ravi marks as DISPOSED in the app. StockLedger logs DAMAGE_WRITE_OFF. Done.
Option B — Return to Supplier: These were defective from the factory. Ravi raises a Supplier Return. Flow 11 kicks in. QuarantineRecord status → RETURNED_TO_SUPPLIER.
Option C — Repackage and Restock: Sometimes it's just a torn box — tablets are fine. Ravi's team repackages and scans back into bin. qtyDamaged decreases, qtyOnHand increases. QuarantineRecord status → RESTOCKED.

Flow 11: Supplier Return
"This batch was bad from day one"
During receiving, Ravi notices 30 units of a new batch of Vitamin C have clearly defective packaging — swollen tablets, wrong colour. The whole sub-lot is suspect.
He rejects those 30 units during inbound. The system does NOT create a BinStock record for them.
Instead it creates a SupplierReturn record:

PO reference: PO-00456
Reason: DEFECTIVE
Quantity: 30 units
Status: RAISED

The supplier is notified. They acknowledge — status moves to ACKNOWLEDGED. Two weeks later they send a credit note — status moves to CREDIT_RECEIVED. Or they send a replacement batch — status moves to REPLACED and Flow 1 runs again for the replacement.

Flow 12: Order Cancellation — Stock Release
"Priya changed her mind before we picked"
Priya placed an order — 10 units were reserved from BATCH-2025-002. Twenty minutes later, before the pick list was even acted on, she cancels.
The system immediately:

Finds the BinStock records that had reservations for her order
Releases the 10 units — qtyReserved goes down by 10, qtyOnHand goes back up by 10
Logs RESERVATION_RELEASED in StockLedger
Those units are immediately available for the next customer

No warehouse staff had to do anything. No physical movement happened. Just a number adjustment.

Flow 13: Partial Fulfillment
"We can only send part of your order"
A pharmacy chain orders 200 units of Vitamin C. The system runs batch selection. After checking all valid BinStocks across the warehouse, it can only find 155 expiry-valid units.
The system has a configuration for Vitamin C — allowPartial = true.
So it:

Reserves all 155 available units
Marks the order as PARTIALLY_FULFILLED
Sends the pharmacy a notification — "We can fulfill 155 of your 200 units now. Remaining 45 will be fulfilled when new stock arrives."

The pharmacy accepts. 155 units are shipped. A backorder is created for 45 units — it will be auto-fulfilled the moment the next PO is received and stocked.

Flow 14: Multi-Warehouse Fulfillment
"Chennai is out, but Pune has it"
A customer in Hyderabad orders Vitamin C. The system checks the nearest warehouse — Warehouse A, Chennai. After running the expiry check, Chennai only has 5 valid units. Customer ordered 20.
System checks the next nearest warehouse — Warehouse B, Pune. Pune has 80 valid units of BATCH-2025-003, expiry well within safe range.
Pune is 2 days from Hyderabad. Buffer check passes. ✅
The system:

Reserves 5 units from Chennai's BinStock
Reserves 15 units from Pune's BinStock
Generates two pick lists — one for each warehouse
Customer sees one order, one delivery, doesn't know it came from two places

Or if Pune can cover all 20 — system simply routes the entire order to Pune to avoid complexity.

Flow 15: Bin Overflow During Receiving
"There's no room in this bin"
A new shipment arrives — 150 units of BATCH-2025-003. Ravi scans bin A2R3L1. The system checks — A2R3L1 currently has 60 units, max is 200. Free space: 140 units.
But 150 > 140. ❌ Overflow.
The system warns Ravi — "A2R3L1 can only take 140 more units. 10 units will overflow. Please select an overflow bin."
Ravi looks at the suggestions — A2R3L2 is empty, max 200 units. He selects it.
The system:

Creates BinStock record in A2R3L1 for 140 units (fills it to max)
Creates a second BinStock record in A2R3L2 for the remaining 10 units
Same batch, same expiry, two different bins
Both bins are added to Inventory.bins for this product
Both bins get INBOUND_RECEIVE ledger entries


Flow 16: Product Shelf Life Configuration
"Setting the rules before anything else"
Before Vitamin C Tablets can even be received into the warehouse, a product manager sets up the shelf life rules in the system.
She opens Product Configuration → Shelf Life and fills in:

Minimum shelf life to sell: 90 days (if less than 90 days remaining, don't sell it)
Minimum shelf life to receive: 180 days (reject supplier batch if expiring within 6 months)
Expiry alert days: 60 days (warn Ravi 60 days before a batch is about to expire)

These three numbers silently power every expiry decision across the entire system — inbound rejection, order selection, PIN code check, expiry sweep alerts. Without this setup, none of those flows can run correctly.
She can also set different rules for a specific variant — for example, the 1000mg variant might have a longer minimum buffer because it's slower moving.

Flow 17: Inbound Stock Rejection
"We can't accept this batch"
A supplier delivers a batch of Vitamin C. Ravi scans the batch details during receiving. Expiry date on the batch: August 2025. Today is May 2026. Remaining shelf life: 3 months = 90 days.
The system checks against ProductShelfLifeConfig — minimum shelf life to receive is 180 days.
90 < 180. ❌ Rejected.
The system refuses to create a BinStock record. It automatically raises a SupplierReturn with reason NEAR_EXPIRY. The supplier's truck is still at the loading bay. Ravi tells the driver — "We can't accept this batch. It'll be returned."
The supplier is notified through the system. Flow 11 takes over for resolution.

Flow 18: Reports & Stock Visibility
"Ravi needs to see everything"
It's Monday morning. Ravi opens his dashboard. He doesn't go bin-hunting manually — the system surfaces everything he needs.
Expiry Report — "3 batches expiring within 60 days across 5 bins. Review and act."
Ravi sees BATCH-2025-001 in two bins with 18 + 12 units expiring in 28 days. He decides to discount them on the storefront to push sales before they expire.
Low Stock Report — "Vitamin D is below reorder point warehouse-wide."
He reviews and confirms the auto-generated PO.
Dead Stock Report — "Omega-3 capsules have had zero outbound movement in 90 days."
He flags it for a promotional campaign.
Bin Utilisation Report — "12 bins are under 20% capacity."
He identifies consolidation opportunities.
Stock Value Report — Total inventory value across all warehouses, broken down by product category.
All of these are read-only queries. No stock moves. Just visibility.

Flow 19: Bin Consolidation
"Free up a bin by merging"
Ravi's dashboard shows a suggestion — "Bin A2R3L1 has 15 units of BATCH-2025-002. Bin A2R4L2 has 60 units of the same batch. A2R4L2 has max 200 units (140 free). You can consolidate A2R3L1 into A2R4L2 and free up bin A2R3L1 for a new product."
Ravi checks — is A2R3L1's stock reserved for any pending order? No. ✅ Safe to move.
He taps Approve Consolidation.
The system:

Moves 15 units from A2R3L1's BinStock to A2R4L2's BinStock (same batch, same expiry — clean merge)
A2R3L1's BinStock → qtyOnHand = 0, isActive = false
A2R3L1 bin → currentStock = 0, isOccupied = false
A2R4L2's BinStock → qtyOnHand = 75
Logs CONSOLIDATION_OUT for A2R3L1 and CONSOLIDATION_IN for A2R4L2
Removes A2R3L1 from Inventory.bins
Creates a StockTransfer record with type CONSOLIDATION

A2R3L1 is now completely empty. Ravi can assign it to an entirely different product tomorrow — maybe a new product line that just got onboarded.
No product was wasted. No order was disrupted. One bin was efficiently recycled.
